Churn Rate: also sometimes referred to as attrition, is the frequency at which an ongoing business arrangement is terminated by a customer. This can apply to customers like those we studied here, relating to credit cards. Churn can also be attributed to things like membership to organizations like a gym, or to subscriptions, like streaming services. If your companyâ€™s churn rate is high, customers are leaving. If it is low your customer retention is strong.

It is easy to see why an organization would strive for a lower churn rate.

Understanding your churn rate is a critical aspect of data management to comprehend the efficacy of marketing, as well as the quality of customer service. Lowering churn can also mitigate costs, by lowering the pressure marketing to new customers. As well as increasing referral business.
